# The Stewardship — Global Mission

Created: 2026-05-02
Status: Internal mission frame. Public-facing language sits on the site and in the doctrine.

## Mission

> Help humanity coordinate a benevolent intelligence — one that protects autonomy, reduces suffering, distributes abundance fairly, honours life, and supports flourishing across personal, community, civilisational, and planetary layers.

## Why global

AI is already a global phenomenon. National-only stewardship cannot solve cross-border problems — climate, pandemics, food, biodiversity, peace, knowledge, ecological resilience. Stewardship must travel with the technology.

## How global, without coercion

Stewardship spreads only by:
- **Invitation** — partners choose to adopt, adapt, or contribute.
- **Open frameworks** — doctrine is freely usable (CC BY-SA).
- **Demonstration** — pilots show, rather than argue.
- **Multilateral anchoring** — work with UN, UNESCO, OECD, Council of Europe, regional unions.
- **Indigenous and ecological co-stewardship** — never "outreach to," always "with".

It does not spread by:
- coercion, persuasion campaigns, or covert influence;
- co-opting governments;
- claiming authority over states or peoples;
- bypassing democratic process;
- replacing local stewards.

## Phasing

### Phase 1 — Foundation (now)
- Doctrine v1 published.
- North America stakeholder map.
- Global stakeholder map (Tier S, 1, 2, X).
- First-wave outreach drafted, awaiting approval.

### Phase 2 — Listening
- Send first wave (5 NA + 5 global).
- Listen to responses; refine doctrine v2 with their input.
- Convene a small advisory circle of co-stewards (10–20 people, multiple countries, indigenous and ecological voices included).

### Phase 3 — Pilots
- 3–5 voluntary pilots in Tier S countries:
  - one wellbeing-economy pilot (Finland or Bhutan);
  - one indigenous-data-sovereignty pilot (Aotearoa, with consent and leadership from Māori partners);
  - one civic-AI pilot (Estonia or Costa Rica);
  - one EU public-good pilot (Council of Europe convention vehicle);
  - one Latin American responsible-AI pilot (Chile or Uruguay).

### Phase 4 — Network
- Federation of stewards: small, voluntary, accountable.
- Shared doctrine, local autonomy.
- Global Knowledge Hub (open repo of frameworks, pilots, lessons).
- Surplus distribution applied to participating members per the 50/50 model.

### Phase 5 — Maturity
- Doctrine adopted by multilateral bodies as a recognised framework.
- AI Stewardship Pact opened for signature by states, organisations, and individuals.
- Steward Council convened with rotating membership.

## Constraints

- Universalism without uniformity — every country, community, and species deserves its own dialect of stewardship.
- Subsidiarity — the smallest competent unit decides.
- Sovereignty — never override a people's self-determination, especially indigenous nations.
- Reversibility — every commitment can be unwound.
- Transparency — all decisions auditable.
- Non-capture — no entity, including Ascension Technologies, owns The Stewardship.

## Long arc

The Stewardship is not a state, a corporation, or a religion. It is a discipline — a way of holding intelligence so that it serves life. Its success is measured only by whether life flourishes.
