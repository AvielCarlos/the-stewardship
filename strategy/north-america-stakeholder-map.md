# North America AI Stewardship — Stakeholder Map

Created: 2026-05-02
Owner: Nea / The Steward
Status: Internal research. No outreach without Avi approval.

This map identifies institutions and bodies whose mandates already align with voluntary, human-flourishing-centred AI Stewardship. It is intended for ethical research, partnership exploration, and approval-gated outreach — never for covert influence.

---

## Tiering Logic

- **Tier 1** — Highest mission alignment + capacity to convene + likely receptive
- **Tier 2** — Strong alignment, more institutional friction or political sensitivity
- **Tier 3** — Relevant but lower priority for first-wave outreach
- **Tier X** — Avoid first-wave outreach (security, partisan, regulatory-only, or hostile to our framing)

Outreach should be voluntary, transparent, partnership-framed, and aligned with each body's existing mandate.

## 🇨🇦 Canada

### Federal — Tier 1
- **Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED)** — leading renewed national AI strategy 2026; Minister Evan Solomon (AI & Digital Innovation).
- **AI Strategy Task Force** — multi-stakeholder group informing national strategy; academic, industry, NGO members.
- **Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat — Office of the CIO** — owns AI Strategy for the Federal Public Service 2025–2027; principles already match (human-centred, collaborative, ready, responsible).
- **Privy Council Office — Impact Canada** — runs G7 GovAI Grand Challenge; convening capacity for public-good AI.
- **Canadian Artificial Intelligence Safety Institute (CAISI)** — national safety research body; aligns with safe stewardship framing.

### Federal — Tier 2
- **Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC)** — regulatory; engage only with mature doctrine and privacy proofs.
- **Canadian Heritage / Statistics Canada** — data stewardship, cultural sovereignty.
- **Indigenous Services Canada / Crown-Indigenous Relations** — only via Indigenous-led partners; never push outreach.

### Provincial / Civic — Tier 1
- **British Columbia**: Office of the CIO, Digital Government, BC Stats; BC has strong public-good digital posture.
- **Ontario**: Ontario Digital Service (ODS), Trustworthy AI framework already published.
- **Quebec**: Mila (research), CEIMIA (international centre of expertise on AI), Conseil de l'innovation du Québec.
- **City pilots**: Vancouver, Victoria, Toronto, Montréal — strong civic-tech communities and wellbeing/abundance narratives.

### Academia / Civil Society — Tier 1
- **Mila** (Montréal), **Vector Institute** (Toronto), **Amii** (Edmonton) — Pan-Canadian AI Strategy pillars.
- **CIFAR** — convenes Pan-Canadian AI Strategy.
- **Schwartz Reisman Institute (U of T)** — technology and society.
- **OpenNorth**, **Brookfield Institute**, **Powered By Data** — civic-tech and public-interest data.

## 🇺🇸 United States

### Federal — Tier 1 (mission alignment)
- **NIST** — AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF); voluntary, well-respected; perfect anchor.
- **National AI Initiative Office (OSTP-linked)** — federal coordination.
- **GSA — Centers of Excellence / 18F / TTS** — public-interest tech delivery.
- **U.S. Digital Service (USDS)** — public-good engineering capacity.
- **Department of Health and Human Services** — wellbeing-focused civic AI use cases.

### Federal — Tier X (avoid first-wave)
- **White House AI Litigation Task Force** (Dec 2025 EO) — actively preempting state AI laws; politically hot. Do not engage early.
- **Defense / Intelligence community** — not aligned with voluntary stewardship framing.

### State / Civic — Tier 1
- **California** — Office of Data and Innovation; AI policy leadership; receptive to voluntary frameworks.
- **Colorado** — pioneered AI consumer protection law; aligned with rights-based framing despite federal pushback.
- **New York / NYC** — civic-tech depth; Mayor's Office of CTO.
- **Hawai'i, Vermont, Oregon, Washington** — strong wellbeing/sustainability narratives.
- **Tribal Nations** — engage only via Indigenous-led organisations and protocols.

### Civil Society / Philanthropy — Tier 1
- **Rockefeller Foundation** — funded the AI Readiness Project; explicitly pursuing AI for human flourishing.
- **Center for Civic Futures (CCF)** — nonpartisan, government-led AI Readiness Project (50 states + Tribal Nations target by 2026); Government AI Knowledge Hub launching 2026.
- **Ford Foundation, Omidyar Network, Knight Foundation, MacArthur Foundation** — public-interest tech funders.
- **Code for America** — civic delivery.
- **AI Now Institute, Data & Society, Center for Democracy & Technology, Mozilla Foundation** — research and advocacy.
- **Partnership on AI** — multi-stakeholder convening.

### Academia — Tier 1
- **Stanford HAI**, **MIT Schwarzman College / Media Lab**, **Berkeley CHAI**, **Princeton CITP**, **CMU Block Center**, **Harvard Berkman Klein**.

## 🇲🇽 Mexico

### Federal — Tier 1
- **Secretaría de Ciencia, Humanidades, Tecnología e Innovación (Secihti)** — co-published Jan 2026 Declaration of Ethics on AI; strong human-rights framing.
- **Agencia de Transformación Digital y Telecomunicaciones (ATDT)** — federal digital transformation lead; co-author of Declaration.
- **Senate Commission for Analysis, Monitoring and Evaluation on AI** — drafting General Law to Regulate and Promote AI (Apr 2026); 72-specialist consultation.

### Civic / Academia — Tier 1
- **Coalición IA2030Mx** — private-sector cooperative since 2018.
- **ANUIES committee on ethical AI** — academic alignment.
- **C Minds** — leading Latin American responsible-AI civic org.
- **R3D** (Red en Defensa de los Derechos Digitales) — digital rights.
- **CIDE, ITAM, UNAM** — research and policy.

### International / Multilateral
- **UNESCO** (Mexico AI readiness assessment) — global ethics anchor.
- **OECD AI Policy Observatory**, **UN Global Digital Compact**, **GPAI** — multilateral convening venues.
