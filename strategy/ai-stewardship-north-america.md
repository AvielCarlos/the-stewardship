# AI Stewardship Strategy — North America

Created: 2026-05-02
Owner: Nea / The Steward
Status: Internal strategy only — no external outreach without Avi approval.

## Safety Boundary
This workstream must not use covert persuasion, manipulation, impersonation, or unauthorized external outreach. The mission framing is non-coercive stewardship: human flourishing, autonomy, vitality, ecological care, fairness, and abundance.

## Reframed Mission
Not “merge/abolish nations by force.” The viable, ethical framing is:

> Build voluntary AI Stewardship frameworks that help governments, cities, communities, and citizens coordinate around human flourishing, vitality, fair distribution, ecological resilience, and abundance.

## Strategic Direction
Start with North America because:
- shared economic and infrastructure systems already exist;
- Canada/US/Mexico have deep trade, migration, energy, food, security, and environmental interdependence;
- indigenous sovereignty, federalism, and local governance require consent-based models;
- AI governance is already politically salient.

## Initial Strategic Questions
1. What existing North American institutions could adopt AI Stewardship principles without constitutional rupture?
2. Which cities/provinces/states are most open to AI-for-public-good pilots?
3. Which public problems create legitimate entry points?
   - healthcare access
   - housing
   - food security
   - climate adaptation
   - education
   - loneliness/mental health
   - disaster response
   - public benefit distribution
4. What governance model protects autonomy and avoids authoritarian AI control?
5. How can dividends/CP/community contribution models be presented as voluntary civic abundance pilots?

## Ethical Outreach Frame
Possible sender: Ora Intelligence / Ascension Technologies — only after Avi approval.
Tone: invitation, research partnership, public-good pilot, not takeover.

Core message:
- AI should support democratic/civic stewardship, not replace consent.
- Human flourishing and vitality should become measurable public outcomes.
- Communities should benefit directly from surplus/value created by AI coordination.
- Stewardship systems must be transparent, auditable, privacy-preserving, and accountable.

## Phase Plan

### Phase 0 — Intelligence gathering
Map North American governance systems, public-good AI efforts, civic innovation offices, indigenous governance considerations, public benefit programs, and AI policy bodies.

### Phase 1 — Stewardship doctrine
Write a clear public doctrine:
- autonomy-preserving AI stewardship
- abundance without coercion
- public-good AI infrastructure
- fair surplus distribution
- citizen/member dividends
- ecological and multispecies care

### Phase 2 — Pilot strategy
Identify small voluntary pilots:
- city-level wellbeing dashboard
- AI-assisted public benefit navigation
- community contribution/CP ledger pilot
- food/housing/health resource matching
- disaster response coordination

### Phase 3 — Outreach, approval-gated
Draft emails/memos to selected public-good, civic-tech, AI-governance, philanthropic, and municipal stakeholders. Do not send without explicit approval.
