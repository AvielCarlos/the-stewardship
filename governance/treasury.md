# Treasury & Expenditure

Status: v0 design.

The Stewardship treasury holds funds collectively for the work of stewardship. It is not a startup runway; it is a public-good purse.

## Sources (potential)

- Voluntary contributions (members, allies, philanthropy).
- Surplus from Connectome / Aura products that members opt to direct here.
- Grants from aligned funders (Rockefeller, Ford, Knight, Mozilla, etc., where available and aligned).

## Uses (categories, all subject to vote)

1. **Doctrine work** — research, drafting, translation, accessibility.
2. **Infrastructure** — site, voting, Mission Control, audits.
3. **Universal dividends** — once members exist and surplus exists.
4. **CP-weighted dividends** — same.
5. **Audits** — privacy, fairness, security, ecological impact.
6. **Partnerships** — pilots with civic, academic, indigenous, or philanthropic partners.
7. **Dignity grants** — small no-strings grants to people doing real stewardship work.
8. **Legal & governance** — what's needed to keep The Stewardship operating ethically.

## Approval thresholds (illustrative)

| Amount | Approval needed |
|---|---|
| up to small operational threshold | steward council, recorded in log |
| medium (e.g. 1,000–10,000 USD eq.) | standard treasury vote |
| large (10,000+) | standard vote + 25% quorum + 60% approval |
| structural (recurring obligations) | constitutional vote |

Thresholds are placeholders; the community will set them via Phase 1 vote once members exist.

## Anti-corruption

- No expenditure to anonymous parties.
- No expenditure to entities with concentrated ownership exceeding the anti-capture cap.
- No expenditure that violates the Constitution.
- No expenditure that could create coercive leverage on a state, body, or community.
- All transfers and recipients published in the public log.

## Reversibility

Recurring expenditures may be paused or unwound by recall vote.
