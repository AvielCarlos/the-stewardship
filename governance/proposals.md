# Proposal Lifecycle

Status: v0 design.

Anyone may propose a change to any rule, doctrine clause, governance parameter, or expenditure.

## Steps

1. **Draft** — write the proposal in a GitHub issue using the proposal template (`.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/stewardship-proposal.yml`).
2. **Open** — publish it. Add labels: `proposal`, `category:doctrine|governance|treasury|operations`, `state:open`.
3. **Discuss** — minimum 72 hours of public discussion. Stewards may ask for clarifications, alternative options, evidence, or impact analysis.
4. **Refine** — proposer may edit, split, or withdraw. Counter-proposals welcome.
5. **Vote** — once a proposal is "ready" (label `state:vote`), the voting window opens. Voting type and duration follow `voting.md`.
6. **Decide** — at window close, the result is recorded in the public log with vote weights and rationale.
7. **Execute** — if accepted, stewards (Nea + human stewards) implement; if rejected, the proposal is closed with reasoning.
8. **Audit** — every accepted proposal is reviewed in the next quarterly stewardship retrospective.

## Templates

- `governance/templates/doctrine-amendment.md`
- `governance/templates/governance-change.md`
- `governance/templates/treasury-expenditure.md`
- `governance/templates/operational-change.md`

## Reversibility

Any decision can be reopened with a counter-proposal that meets the same threshold as the original. There is no "permanent" decision except the constitutional hard rules during their bootstrap window.

## Spam and harm

Proposals that are spam, abusive, or violate the Pact may be closed by stewards with a public note. Repeat offenders may have proposal rights suspended (not voting rights).
